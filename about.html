About me

Back when I was a 5th grade teacher, one of the lessons that I remember the most was teaching the 50 states and capitals. Memorization was not everyone’s forté, so I decided to break up the task into smaller chunks. With the students that that still had trouble, I broke up the learning goals even more. After a few weeks, I was able to get most of my class to pass the tests. This helped me learn that not every problem can be taken head on. Much like creating a multi-step user flow, I knew that breaking up a complex task into manageable chunks would be more fruitful than trying to memorize all 50 states at once.

A few years later I had stepped away from classroom teaching, but still found myself refining lessons- this time at an Apple store. Having done my fair share of setups I noticed some people consistently got stuck at the same points in the setup process. A screen would teach them one way to interact with the device and then change this process on the next. Unable to change the software I helped people look for ways the software was showing them how to continue. Knowing now what I know about the UX process - user testing these few screens would have proven invaluable. This data would have allowed me to change my prototype to make sure different users could move on without any issues.

And while during the day I worked, at night I was a full-time DJ and music producer. After some time I began to DJ less and had less. At work, I didn’t express my creativity and that coupled with my schedule led to a drastic decline in my desire to make music. I knew I needed a creative outlet to help me stay balanced, so I looked at options that could afford me creativity but also a career. A friend I had mentioned that I look into UX and within a few clicks of a web search I knew that’s what I wanted to do. I had the perfect way to show my ingenuity and problem solving in one amazing career. I could take my vast experiences helping people achieve their goals and use design principles to translate that into digital products. I have been dedicated to becoming a UX Designer ever since and I look forward to creating products people love to use.
